Part of the breach fun-pass flow (spike

## What went well

- Every piece reused a proven crate module (emissive `TempEntity` meshes like the
  existing tracer for the muzzle flash / spawn beacon; `ScreenFlash` for the low-HP
  throb; a `BorderColor` box for the hit marker), so the change was mechanical and
  low-risk -- no new machinery.
- Reading `ScreenFlash`'s fields BEFORE writing the low-HP pulse caught the one real
  trap: `Default` is `despawn_on_end: true`, which would have despawned the *persistent*
  `DamageVignette` overlay after the first throb and silently broken every later hit
  flash. Matching `enemy_melee`'s `despawn_on_end: false` avoided it. This is exactly the
  "check the plugin/type's build/fields before assuming defaults" habit flagged in the
  combos retro paying off a second time.
- Keeping the interactive juice off a resource flag (`HitFlash`) rather than an entity
  event meant the hit-marker fade is a trivial pure-ish system, and the one genuinely
  pure bit (`is_low_health`, incl. the zero-max guard) got a unit test.

## What went wrong

- The visual layer could not be verified by screenshot: a `BCS_SHOT` capture of the
  Playing state came back fully black -- even the always-on white crosshair dot was
  missing, so it was the known headless offscreen-capture unreliability
  (13_glide/reactor retros), not a content bug. Worse, a state-entry screenshot never
  drives the fire-triggered juice (hit marker, muzzle flash) anyway. Net: for
  input-triggered visual polish there is no cheap headless "see it" -- I leaned on the
  no-B0004 autopilot run, the pure test, and the despawn_on_end fix, and said so plainly
  in the review rather than implying visual confirmation.

## What to improve next time

- For input-triggered visual juice, do not budget on a screenshot proving it; the
  reliable signals are (a) the autopilot run is clean of B0004 / despawn errors, (b) any
  logic is pure and tested, and (c) the known traps (unlit-kills-bloom,
  despawn_on_end-kills-persistent-overlay, absolute-not-accumulating shake) are checked
  by reading the module. Spend the verification effort there, not on a flaky grab.
- When re-spiking a persistent overlay entity (vs spawning a one-shot), always pass the
  non-despawning variant explicitly -- the ergonomic default is the one-shot.
